Pricing: Pay half, or pay once

Dex charges $12-20/month ($144-240/year) with no lifetime option. That adds up to over $700 over five years for what is essentially a contacts database.

Nexus Pro is $6/month — exactly half the cost. Or you can grab a $99 lifetime license (limited to the first 500 users) and never pay again. Both plans include unlimited contacts, unlimited AI actions, Google + Outlook sync, and cloud backup.

Privacy: Your contacts, your device

Dex stores all your relationship data on their servers. If they go down, get acquired, or change their terms, your data goes with them.

Nexus is a desktop app with a local SQLite database. Your contacts live on your computer — always accessible, even offline. Cloud sync is optional, only for backup and multi-device access.

AI: Yours to keep, not rented

Dex added AI features in 2026 (Dex Shuffle, Claude/ChatGPT integration), but they're gated behind the $20/month Professional tier. You're renting access to AI you can't control.

Nexus takes a different approach: bring your own API key (BYOK). You pay Anthropic directly for what you use (typically a few cents per conversation), with no subscription markup. Your Copilot can answer questions about your network, generate reconnection messages, create meeting prep briefings, and suggest tags — all without a monthly fee.

Where Dex still wins

To be fair, Dex has advantages. It's a web app, so you can access it from any browser on any device without installing anything. Their Chrome extension is polished and well-established. And they have a larger user community and more integrations.

If browser-based access is critical for your workflow and you don't mind the price, Dex is a solid choice. But if you value privacy, offline access, AI features, and keeping more money in your pocket, Nexus is the better option.
