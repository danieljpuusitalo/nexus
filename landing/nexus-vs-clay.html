AI: Both have it, different approaches

Clay pioneered AI in personal CRMs with automatic contact enrichment and relationship insights. Their AI pulls data from public sources to fill in details about your contacts.

Nexus takes a different approach. Instead of enrichment, the AI Copilot lets you query your entire network conversationally — "Who do I know in fintech?", "When did I last talk to Sarah?" It also generates reconnection messages, meeting prep briefings, and suggests tags. Your data never leaves your machine unless you explicitly use the AI features.

Pricing: 40% cheaper with a lifetime option

Clay charges $10/month ($120/year). Over three years, that's $360 for a contacts app.

Nexus Pro is $6/month ($72/year). Or pay $99 once and own it forever — that's less than one year of Clay. The lifetime deal is limited to 500 users, and includes all future updates.

Views Nexus has that Clay doesn't

Nexus includes a pipeline/kanban board that automatically sorts contacts by how recently you've been in touch. There's also an interactive map view showing contacts by location (great for travel planning), a network graph visualizing your relationship web, and a keep-in-touch system with frequency buckets. Clay doesn't offer any of these views.

Where Clay still wins

Clay's automatic contact creation from email is genuinely useful — it passively builds your CRM without manual entry. Their mobile app is also mature, and their AI enrichment can save time filling in details.

If passive contact creation and mobile access are essential to your workflow, Clay may be worth the premium. But if you prefer control over your data, more visualization options, and significantly lower cost, Nexus is the stronger choice.
